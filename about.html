About BioFest

About BioFest.

BioFest is a two-day Biology Festival where high school students who are not yet familiar with the branches of Biology can delve deeper into Bioinformatics, Neuroscience, and Medicine.

On the first day of BioFest, the first leg of the symposium will begin as pioneer professors within their field give interactive lectures regarding their research, while giving students an opportunity to ask questions about the field and the methodolgy for research within each field. This way, students will be able to widen their network.

At the end of day 1, as the second leg of the symposium being a contest, groups of students will be introduced to a case study and they will be asked to submit an outline of how they would approach the case study and the specific questions presented.

On the second day, students will be asked to meet with their groups via zoom calls where they will prepare a presentation introducing and solving the case study. The jury responsible for selecting three winners will enter some of these zoom calls to understand the approach the students are taking. After finishing up the preparations of the participants, each group will be asked to present their finished product to the jury. Consequently, the jury will choose a winner.

The winning team will be awarded a special prize and at the end of day two, an esteemed research fellow studying abroad will give a lecture at the closing ceremony and talk about how biological research is conducted abroad. Students will be given stamped certificates with their degree. (Detailed schedule will be announced)
